EDITA IWA (SLICED TAPIOCA)

This is a snack made from cassava. It can be eaten with cocount, steamed fish or salted fish (kippers), 'lsip eyop' (palm fruit nuts), salted pork (dried in the sun and over 'Utang', the over head local oven.

Ingredients

  • Cassava

Instructions

  1. Cut yam (or plantain) into thick cubes, wash and put aside

  2. Peel the cassava and wash. Boil till it is cooked but firm. Remove from boiling water into cool water.

  3. With a sharp knife, slice the cassava into strips of your desired thickness and size. The thinner the strips, the better

  4. Soak the cut cassava in cool water and leave to stand, prefarably for several hours or over night. Strain and rinse the cassava and the Edita Iwa is ready to be eaten.

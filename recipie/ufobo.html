UFOBO MMONG BIA OR UKOM (YAM OR PLANTAIN PEPPERSOUP)

This is a broth (pepper soup) prepared with goat meat or other meat of choice like chicken or fish. It is usually made to be a bit spicy and is a great meal to have in a cold season. It is also good for people recovering from a cold or flu. The yam or plantain makes it filling. It is also important to mention that the goat that is smoked is the common variety used for this meal, as opposed to the one scraped with a razor blade. The smoked one adds a distinct barbecue flavour to the pepper soup.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium sized yam tuber or 4 fingers slightly ripe plantain
  • Pieces of goat meat
  • 1 onion
  • Fresh pepper (scotch bonnet)
  • 3 cooking spoons of palm oil
  • Dry yellow pepper
  • Blended pepper soup spices
  • 3 stock cubes
  • Ntong (scent) leaves
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Cut yam (or plantain) into thick cubes, wash and put aside

  2. Wash the goat meat throughly and put in a pot to boil. Blend the onion and fresh pepper together and add to the boiling meat. Add 2 stock cubes and salt. Allow the meat to start cooking in its own juices. This is to allow the absorption of flavours into the meat before adding water. Add more water when the stock reduces. Allow to cook for about 20 minutes or till tender. Add some dry yellow pepper for flavour, peppersoup spices and an additional stock cube.

  3. While boiling, add your yam (or plantain) and cook together till the yam is soft. The yam cooks quickly, that is why you donâ€™t add it at the beginning

  4. Add sliced ntong and taste for salt. Adjust accordingly. Continue cooking for about 3 minutes and your ufobo mmong bia is ready.

Indy's tips: For the pepper soup to cook quicker, it is advisable to use the male variety of the goat for your cooking. It gets tender faster than the female variety and provides great aroma.

BALANGU

Balangu refers to grilled ram meat that is very popular in Northern Nigeria, but eaten all over Nigeria. Balangu, sometimes called ‘balango’, is boneless beef grilled in its fat. Balangu is a suya alternative. The meat is usually roasted without seasoning first to smoke out the natural flavor of the meat before adding salt and spices in later stages. Balangu is more moist and tender and sometimes focused on particular roasted parts of the meat. As street food, balangu is served in brown paper or newspaper together with onions or rice cake (masa).

IPEKERE (PLANTAIN CHIPS)

IPEKERE ARE AS CRUNCHY AS CHIN-CHIN, AND THEY MAKE A PERFECT SNACK TO SNEAK IN YOUR HANDBAG OR EAT ON A ROAD TRIP

KITCHEN TOOLS YOU NEED

  • Frying Pan
  • Sharp knife
  • Kitchen stove
  • Oven
  • Strainer
  • Paper Towels
  • Baking Sheet

INGREDIENTS

  • 2-3 unripe or semi-ripe plantains
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • Powdered pepper (optional)
  • Ginger powder (optional)
  • Garlic powder (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cut both ends of the plantain and peel them.

  2. Horizontally or diagonally, cut thin slices, about 0.5cm.

  3. Sprinkle salt and spiced powders of preference over the slices.

TO FRY:

  1. In hot oil, fry for about 2-3 minutes
  2. Scoop out and let it cool in a strainer lined with paper towels.

TO BAKE:

  1. Heat up your oven to 190°C
  2. Over the seasoned plantains, pour 2 tablespoons of oil and mix with the plantain to avoid sticking in the pan. You may also use baking parchment paper (with no oil).
  3. Arrange the slices on the baking sheet. Bake for about 18 minutes or until they start turning brown on the edges.
  4. Eat cold or with salsa. Enjoy!

Frikkadel

"Frikkadel is a delightful South African dish featuring savory meatballs. These meatballs are seasoned to perfection and often served with a sweet and sour sauce. Enjoy them as a main dish or as part of a flavorful spread."

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 slices of white bread, soaked in water and squeezed dry
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, finely chopped onion, and soaked bread.
  2. Add milk, Worcestershire sauce, beaten egg, chopped parsley, ground coriander, ground cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into small meatballs, about the size of a golf ball.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
  5. Carefully place the meatballs in the hot oil and fry until golden brown on all sides. Ensure that they are cooked through.
  6. Remove the meatballs from the pan and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  7. Serve the Frikkadel hot, either on their own or with a sweet and sour sauce.
Tips:
  • Experiment with different ground meats like beef, lamb, or a combination of both.
  • Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preferences.
  • Serve Frikkadel with a side of mashed potatoes or rice for a complete meal.

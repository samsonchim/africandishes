Bunny Chow

"Bunny Chow is a South African fast-food dish consisting of a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry. It originated in the Indian community of Durban and has become a popular street food in South Africa. This delicious and hearty dish is perfect for sharing with friends and family."

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of unsliced white bread (round or long)
  • 500g chicken or lamb, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Cut the top off the loaf of bread and hollow out the center, creating a bread bowl. Save the bread chunks for serving.
  2. In a large pan, saut√© the chopped onion until translucent.
  3. Add the chicken or lamb pieces and brown them on all sides.
  4. Stir in the curry powder, ground turmeric, ground cumin, chili powder, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they start to break down.
  6. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, season with salt and pepper, and simmer until the meat is cooked through and the flavors meld together.
  7. Spoon the curry mixture into the hollowed-out bread loaf.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Serve the Bunny Chow hot, with the reserved bread chunks on the side for dipping and scooping.
Tips:
  • Adjust the spice level according to your preference by adding more or less chili powder.
  • You can use a round or long loaf of bread depending on your preference or availability.
  • Feel free to customize the filling with your choice of protein or make it vegetarian.

Amasi

"Amasi is a fermented milk product, commonly enjoyed in various African countries. It is known for its tangy flavor and creamy texture. Amasi can be consumed on its own or used in cooking and baking."

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon plain yogurt with live cultures

Instructions

  1. Heat the milk in a saucepan until it reaches a temperature of about 110°F (43°C).
  2. Allow the milk to cool slightly, then add the plain yogurt with live cultures. Stir well to combine.
  3. Cover the saucepan with a lid and wrap it in a towel or blanket to maintain warmth. Let it sit undisturbed for 12-24 hours to allow the fermentation process to take place.
  4. Check for a thickened consistency and a tangy flavor. Once achieved, refrigerate the amasi to stop the fermentation process.
  5. Serve chilled and enjoy!
Tips:
  • Ensure the yogurt used contains live cultures for successful fermentation.
  • Adjust the fermentation time based on your desired thickness and tanginess.
  • Store the amasi in the refrigerator and consume within a week.

Chakalaka

"Chakalaka is a spicy and tangy vegetable relish that originated in South Africa. It is a flavorful accompaniment to various dishes, adding a burst of vibrant taste to your meal. Enjoy it as a side dish or as a condiment."

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 can (400g) baked beans in tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Saut√© the chopped onion until softened.
  3. Add minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Stir in diced green and red bell peppers, and grated carrot. Cook until vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they start to break down.
  6. Pour in baked beans in tomato sauce and mix well.
  7. Season with curry powder, ground paprika, ground cumin, sugar, salt, and pepper. Adjust seasoning according to taste.
  8. Simmer the mixture for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  9. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving.
Tips:
  • Chakalaka can be served hot or cold, making it a versatile side dish or condiment.
  • Feel free to customize the level of spiciness by adjusting the amount of curry powder or adding chili if desired.
  • Experiment with additional vegetables like cabbage or beans for extra texture and flavor.

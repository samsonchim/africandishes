Ugali

"Ugali is the most common staple food in Kenya. It is a polenta-like dish, made from maize, millet or sorghum flour, added to boiling water and cooked until it becomes a dense block. It is usually eaten with meat stew, Nyama Choma (Roasted beef or mutton) or most commonly with Sukuma Wiki (cooked kale). It is a well-known energy-giving and body-building food. "

Ingredients

  • 1 cup maize (corn) flour
  • 2 cups water

Instructions

  1. Boil water until it bubbles. The water should be very hot. This ensures the Ugali will cook perfectly

  2. Add a cup of maize flour into the water. Let it cook a few seconds until water starts to cover the flour

  3. Use a wooden spoon to quickly start mixing the flour and water

  4. Add a handful of flour and continue to mix the water and flour it will start to come together

  5. Reduce heat to medium and keep turning the Ugali as it continues to stick together

  6. Gather the Ugali and press it onto the side of the Cooking pot

  7. Place the wooden spoon under the Ugali and form into a ball in the middle of the cooking pot

  8. Repeat pressing the Ugali on the side of the cooking pot and turning it to the middle

  9. Once the Ugali has become firm, turn it once more and smoothen it into a round. You will know your Ugali is ready once you start to smell the aroma clearly

  10. Place the cooked Ugali over onto a plate

  11. Dip the wooden spoon into water and use it to smooth the Ugali into a ball on the plate

  12. Serve the Ugali whole or sliced. Enjoy with your favorite stew, meat, veggies, fish and more

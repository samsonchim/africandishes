Pilau

"A wedding in Kenya without Pilau is a big ‘No-No’. Pilau is a traditional meal among the coastal people of Kenya and Tanzania. It is a fragrant rice dish cooked using whole organic spices, beef, chicken or mutton, and is commonly served with Kachumbari – the Kenya version of Salsa. "

Ingredients

  • 2 cup long grain basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • ½ chicken, sliced into bite-size pieces
  • Vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves, whole
  • 2 cinnamon sticks, whole
  • 5 cardamoms, whole (lightly smash for stronger smell and taste)
  • 9 black peppercorns, whole
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of each ginger and garlic, crushed
  • 2 peeled potatoes (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

  1. In a cooking pot, add onions and vegetable oil and cook until the onions start to brown.

  2. Make a hole in the middle of the flour mixture; add the warm water and start kneading until the water has been absorbed.

  3. Knead the mixture for 5-10 minutes and add flour if needed, until the dough is non-sticky on your bowl and hands.

  4. Add 2-3 tablespoons of vegetable oil and continue kneading until the oil mixes well and the dough feels soft.

  5. Cover the dough and leave it to rest for 20-30 minutes.

  6. On a smooth flat surface, roll out the kneaded dough using a rolling pin (make sure the surface is dusted with flour)

  7. After completely stretching it out, divide it into 8 straight strips using a sharp knife. Coil each of the strips to form a ball- like shape

  8. Dust the flat surface with more flour and take one of the balls and roll it out using a rolling pin to a flat circular shape.

  9. Repeat this process to the rest of the remaining balls.

  10. On a hot pan, place the rolled out circular Chapati and fry (using medium heat) each side with a little bit of vegetable oil until its golden brown. Place your cooked Chapati on a flat plate and cover with aluminum foil or store them in a hot pot. Repeat this step with the rest of the remaining coiled dough.
